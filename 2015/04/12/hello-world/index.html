Hello World!

April 12th, 2015
start

Until now I didn't have the urge to create yet another blog to occupy bits and bytes in the vast space of the internet. Well, things change. Simple proof is, that I am writing this post and sharing it with the world.

The question is - why am I now writing here?.

I'd like to take a short trip down memory lane. I've first came in touch with the internet when I was around 12 years old (the times of dial-up-modems and painfully slow internet connections). Pretty early on I got excited about (web-) programming and started to learn html, css and php (remember when php files ended with php3? I do). I am a self-taught programmer and pretty much everything I learned back then was from online tutorials and discussions. Over the years I've been creating websites, administrating servers and writing software. My projects and working environments challenged me to learn and develop my skills further. During my journy I've learned a lot from attending university classes, reading technical books and talking to colleges. But there is another resource I am frequenly using and for which I am truly grateful: the Internet. For nearly every problem I've encountered so far, I was able to find more information online and to solve it eventually. Whenever I wanted to further educate myself, information was just merely a search away. Basicly speakig , the internet with it's endless free information available via websites, blogs and discussion communities has allowed me to educate myself on many different topics (many of them which are not related with technology and programming).

Having said this, I first want to say thank you to 'the Internet'. By that I mean thank you to everyone who is crontibuting information with their blog posts, tutorials, answers on discussion or by opensourcing their software.

Secondly, this is how I intend to use this blog: I want to give back to the community by sharing some of the things I've learned over the years. This is certainly nothing new for me, I've always written programming diaries for myself, in order to collet solutions for problems I've encountered, or forthings I've learned. Now I am taking it a step further by publishing some of it online in this blog. I don't intend to post daily/weekly or write large tutorial series, I merely want to share now and then some of the things I've learned or encountered.
